CC3. Debating. Use a visualisation in economic/policy commentary

Income inequality translates into opportunity -and outcome- inequalities that affect both individual and colective wellbeing in our societies. From an economic perspective some inequalities are allowed as they signal most productive sectors in the economy. Nevertheless, as John Ralws (1971) argues, and Daniel Chandler supports (listen to debate here) there are core values/principles that shape how fairness in a society looks like.

For inequality to sustainably exist, it has to null in terms of opportunities for basic liberties (freedom of speech/sexuality/religion/politics), and it has to result in redistributions of its "benefits" to the entire population (specially those who allowed such levels of inequality; the most vulnerable).
